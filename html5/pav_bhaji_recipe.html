Pav Bhaji Recipe

Pav Bhaji – The King of Indian Street Food

Pav Bhaji is one of the most popular and mouth-watering street foods of India. Originating from Mumbai, this spicy vegetable mash served with buttered bread rolls has become a favorite snack and meal across the country. Loved by people of all ages, Pav Bhaji is not just food—it's an experience

Origin and History

Pav Bhaji was invented in the 1850s in Mumbai as a quick lunch option for textile mill workers. The dish was originally a mixture of leftover vegetables mashed together with spices and served with pav (soft bread rolls). Over time, it gained popularity and became a common item at street food stalls.

Why It’s So Popular

Pav Bhaji is delicious, spicy, and filling. It is easy to make in large quantities, making it ideal for street vendors. The rich aroma of butter and spices attracts people instantly. It’s also affordable and can be eaten as a snack or a full meal.

Health Factor

While Pav Bhaji is tasty, it is often high in butter and carbs. However, homemade versions can be made healthier by using less butter and whole wheat pav. Since it contains a variety of vegetables, it still offers good nutritional value.

Ingredients
Ingredients Quantity
Potatoes (boiled and mashed) 2 large
Cauliflower florets (boiled and mashed) 1 cup
Green peas (boiled) 1/2 cup
Chopped capsicum (bell pepper) 1/2 cup
Tomatoes (finely chopped) 2 medium
Onion (finely chopped) 1 large
Butter (plus extra for serving) 2 tablespoons
Oil 1 tablespoon
Ginger-garlic paste 1 tablespoon
Pav Bhaji Masala 2 tablespoons
Red chili powder (adjust to taste) 1/2 teaspoon
Turmeric powder 1/4 teaspoon
Salt To taste
Water As needed for consistency
Fresh coriander leaves (for garnish) As needed
Lemon (cut into wedges) 1
Recipe
  • Step 1: Prepare the Vegetables
  • Boil potatoes, cauliflower, and green peas until soft. Mash them together and keep aside.
  • Step 2: Make the Bhaji
  • Heat butter and oil in a pan.
  • Add chopped onions and sauté until golden brown.
  • Add ginger-garlic paste and sauté for 1 minute.
  • Add chopped tomatoes, capsicum, turmeric, red chili powder, and pav bhaji masala.
  • Cook until the tomatoes are soft and oil starts to separate.
  • Add the mashed vegetables and mix well.
  • Add salt and water as needed to get the desired consistency.
  • Let it simmer for 8–10 minutes, stirring occasionally.
  • Garnish with fresh coriander and a small cube of butter on top.
  • Step 3: Toast the Pav
  • Slice pav buns horizontally.
  • Apply butter on a hot tawa (griddle).
  • Toast pav until golden brown on both sides.
